### üî¨ The Anatomy of J-Curve Controls (Technical Deep Dive)
*When the client asks "What exactly does this dropdown change?", use these definitions:*

1.  **Deployment Pacing**: Controls the velocity of capital calls.
    *   *Significance*: Front-loading (e.g., Secondaries) reduces unfunded drag but increases early cash needs. Back-loading (e.g., VC) allows capital to grow elsewhere until needed.
2.  **J-Curve Depth**: Models the magnitude of the initial negative returns (fees + early markdowns).
    *   *Significance*: Infrastructure typically has "Shallow" curves due to early yield. Early Stage VC has "Deep" curves due to high initial burn and long product cycles.
3.  **Time to Breakeven**: The precise point where cumulative net cash flow turns positive.
    *   *Significance*: A key liquidity metric. Strategies with early breakeven (Secondary, Private Credit) provide faster recycling potential.
4.  **Distribution Start & Speed**: Defines the inception and slope of the realization phase.
    *   *Significance*: High speed models "quick flip" or aggressive realization markets. Slow speed models challenging exit environments (e.g., current M&A frost).
